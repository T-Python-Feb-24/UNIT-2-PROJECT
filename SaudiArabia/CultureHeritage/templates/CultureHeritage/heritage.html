Heritage

Heritage is the sum of values, beliefs, literature, arts, and knowledge, all of man’s material and moral activity. It is the result of the accumulation of society’s experiences, and it is a witness to the nation’s history and conditions. It is characterized by being composed of interconnected structures, complete parts, and overlapping many times, some of which are fixed and some of which are variable.

Tangible Heritage

It is everything that is tangible and has a material presence, in addition to those monuments that are important for the study of human history because they provide a tangible basis. The types of tangible cultural heritage are divided into two parts: movable tangible heritage (such as antiques and antiquities), and immovable tangible heritage such as buildings.

Intangible Heritage

It is a heritage that does not have a tangible presence, as it includes customs and traditions, or living expressions inherited from ancestors and passed on to descendants, such as: oral traditions, performing arts, popular and social practices, in addition to the rituals of religious celebrations, and the skills of producing traditional crafts, music, dance, and literature. As for the method of protecting intangible heritage, it lies with the members of society. This is because it is what gives societies their own identity.
